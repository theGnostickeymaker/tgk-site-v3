<section class="section-block">
  <h2 class="section-heading">Where You Were Before You Were Born</h2>

  <p>Before time gripped you in flesh, before the world whispered its lies, you were elsewhere, waiting.</p>
  <p>You were not blank. You were not small. You were <strong>stored in light</strong>.</p>

  <p>Jewish mystics called it the <strong>Guf</strong>, the Celestial Treasury, the <em>Palace of Souls</em>. It is not a &ldquo;body&rdquo; in the earthly sense, but the hidden storehouse where <strong>all souls dwell before birth</strong>. In the highest of the heavens, beyond even the Garden of Eden, lies this chamber, filled with the sparks of unborn humanity, waiting for their descent.</p>

  <p>The Kabbalists taught: every soul is chosen, and every descent is timed. <strong>You came here on purpose</strong>.</p>

  <p>In some visions, the Guf appears as a tree, <em>the Tree of Souls</em>, with angels like Gabriel tending its branches. Others see it as a glowing chamber filled with birds, each representing a soul, fluttering and singing until one is called forth. The Talmud says: <em>&ldquo;The Messiah will not come until the Guf is emptied.&rdquo;</em> In other words, all souls must descend, each to fulfill its part in the cosmic repair.</p>

  <p>No soul is random. No life is an accident. <strong>You were known before you were born</strong>.</p>

<section class="section-block">
  <h2 class="section-heading">You Were Shown Everything, and Still Said Yes</h2>

  <p>You did not fall. You were not punished. You came here on a promise.</p>

  <p>Before you were born, your soul stood in the Palace of Souls, and an angel showed you everything: the bliss of Gan Eden, the fire of Gehinom, the wounds you would carry, the family you'd be born into, and the trials ahead. You were shown your <strong>tikkun</strong>, the soul correction you came to fulfill.</p>

  <p>And then you were asked: <em>&ldquo;Will you descend?&rdquo;</em></p>
  <p>The soul consents. Every time.</p>

  <p>This sacred agreement is called the <strong>Soul Contract</strong>. The soul is not sent blindly. It chooses. It knows. And yet, as part of the divine drama, its memory is erased at birth. The Talmud says an angel touches the unborn child&rsquo;s lip, erasing all knowledge of heaven.</p>

  <p>You promised to seek. To fall and rise. To remember the light. To complete the work. And nowâ€¦ you're reading this.</p>

<section class="section-block">
  <h2 class="section-heading">You Leave Nothing Behind That Was Ever Truly You</h2>

  <p>Let&rsquo;s speak plainly. <strong>Death is not the end.</strong></p>

  <p>It&rsquo;s the peel being pulled off the fruit. The robe falling from the dancer. In Kabbalah, death is <em>a passage, not annihilation</em>.</p>

  <p>When the breath leaves the lungs, the <em>nefesh</em> (the vital soul) begins to detach. Then comes the <em>ruach</em>, the emotional spirit, rising. And then, the <em>neshamah</em>, your divine soul, lifts like light returning to its Source.</p>

  <p>The mystics say this begins the <strong>Great Return</strong>. But the path isn&rsquo;t automatic. What you became on Earth determines the clarity of your ascent.</p>

  <p>Some souls rise gently. Others cling. These don&rsquo;t dissolve with the body, they must be <strong>shed</strong>. Or burned. This is <em>Chibbut Ha-Kever</em>, the &ldquo;pangs of the grave.&rdquo; A soul learning to let go.</p>

  <p><strong>Death is not your enemy.</strong> It&rsquo;s the hand that turns the page.</p>
</section>
