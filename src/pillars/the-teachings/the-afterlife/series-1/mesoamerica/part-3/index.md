  <p>This was known as the <strong>Calendar Round, </strong>the moment when the 260-day sacred calendar (<em>Tzolk&rsquo;in</em>) and the 365-day solar calendar (<em>Haab</em>) realigned.   <p>Because if the gods did not relight the flame at the end of the 52-year cycle, <strong>the world would end</strong>.</p>

  <p>On the final night of the cycle, every fire across the empire was extinguished. Families sat in darkness, fasting and praying.   <p>If the stars moved as expected, and the fire caught, <strong>the world was reborn</strong>.</p>

  <p>The new flame was carried like divine lightning to every home. Life restarted. Time had survived itself.</p>

  <p>But it was more than survival. It was <em>symbolic resurrection</em>. A reminder that death and return were woven into the very structure of time.</p>
